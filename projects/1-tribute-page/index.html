Avicii

One of The World's Greatest DJs

Timeline of Avicii's life

  • 1989 – Born in Stockholm, Sweden as Tim Bergling. Son of Kias Bergling and actress Anki Lidén.
  • 2010 – released the hit song "Seek Bromance", which reached the top 20 in several countries, including Belgium, France, the Netherlands,United Kingdom, and Sweden.
  • 2011 – Released "Levels", which launched him into the mainstream. "Levels" reached the top ten in Austria, Belgium, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Croatia, Denmark, Finland, Germany, Greece, Ireland, Italy, the Netherlands, Slovenia and the United Kingdom, whilst topping the charts in Hungary, Norway and Sweden.
  • 2012 – Collaboration track "Sunshine" with David Guetta was nominated for a Grammy award under the category for Best Dance Recording.
  • 2012 – Made history as the first DJ to headline the world-famous Radio City Music Hall in New York City.
  • 2013 – Nominated for a Grammy for Best Dance Recording with "Levels" at the 2013 Grammy Awards
  • 2013 – Announced and premiered new tracks from his forthcoming new album True.
  • 2013 – Releases Stories
  • 2013 – "Wake Me Up" is premiered and is a hit in many countries
  • 2014 - Worked on many songs for his album.
  • 2015 – Stories is released alongside 3 promotional singles: "Broken Arrows" with Zac Brown, "Ten More Days" with Zak Abel and "Gonna Love Ya" with Sandro Cavazza
  • 2016 – Announced via his Facebook page that he would be retiring from performing later that year, citing health concerns
  • 2016 – Performed his final show at Ushuaïa Ibiza Beach Hotel
  • 2017 – Released the six-track EP Avīci (01)
  • 2017 - Announced a documentary directed by his close and long time collaborator Levan Tsikurishvili, titled Avicii: True Stories. The documentary chronicles the artist's retirement from touring and features interviews from his colleagues David Guetta, Tiësto, Wyclef Jean, Nile Rodgers and Chris Martin of Coldplay.
  • 2018 - Dies near Muscat, Oman at the age of 28
  • 2019 - Posthumous release of album entitled Tim
